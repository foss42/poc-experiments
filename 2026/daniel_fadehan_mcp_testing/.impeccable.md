## Design Context

### Users
A mixed audience including developers connecting to MCP servers, general non-technical users testing tools, and internal enterprise users. The context is generally focused on technical exploration, debugging, and building. The interface should evoke confidence, clarity, and utility.

### Brand Personality
A blend of modern technical precision, minimalist elegance, and touches of playfulness. The interface should feel sharp, precise, and highly usable without being overly rigid or boring. 

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode preferred with a focus on high utility and data density. Clean layouts with clear hierarchy, relying heavily on proper spacing and alignment rather than excessive borders or cards. Minimalist aesthetic.

### Design Principles
1. **Utility First:** Ensure all data, logs, and interactions are immediately accessible and clearly readable. Density should not compromise clarity.
2. **Intentional Rhythm:** Use a consistent spacing scale (tight grouping for related elements, generous separation between distinct sections) to break monotony.
3. **Elegant Minimalism:** Rely on space, weight, and typography to establish visual hierarchy instead of unnecessary nested cards or heavy borders.
4. **Adaptive Structure:** Use appropriate layout mechanisms (Flexbox for 1D, Grid for 2D) to create structures that feel sturdy and responsive.
5. **Clear Wayfinding:** Ensure the "squint test" passes; users should instantly recognize the most important actions and information in any view.
